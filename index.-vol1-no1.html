Middle East

Trump Signals Escalation in Primetime
Address as Allies Reject Hormuz Call

Markets rattle after president offers no exit strategy; Macron calls forced reopening of strait "unrealistic"; over 30 nations convene without the U.S. to discuss shipping crisis

Compiled from wire reports · April 2, 2026

President Trump's primetime address on Tuesday evening failed to calm global anxiety over the Iran war, offering no clear exit strategy and signaling instead that the U.S. would intensify strikes over the next two to three weeks. Markets reversed a brief rally on Wednesday as oil prices surged and stocks sold off sharply — Dow futures fell 670 points, the S&P dropped 1.6%, and the Nasdaq declined 2.1%. The S&P 500 posted its worst quarterly performance since September 2022.

French President Emmanuel Macron declared it "unrealistic" for Trump to urge allies to forcibly reopen the Strait of Hormuz, and more than 30 nations convened virtually on Thursday to discuss measures to restore freedom of navigation — notably without the United States in attendance. Over 100 international law experts signed a statement expressing concerns about violations of international law by all parties.

On the ground, U.S.-Israeli strikes destroyed the B1 bridge near Tehran — described as the tallest bridge in the Middle East — and inflicted what Iranian media called "fundamental destruction" on the Mobarakeh Steel Complex in Isfahan, bringing production to a complete halt. Amnesty International called on all sides to de-escalate strikes on civilian infrastructure. Iran's parliament approved a plan to toll the Strait of Hormuz. An Iranian strike earlier in the week hit a Kuwaiti tanker near Dubai. Gulf states reportedly want the conflict to continue until Iran is no longer a threat, even as Trump told aides privately he is willing to wind things down.

Space

Artemis II Launches — Humans Return to the Moon

NASA launched four astronauts toward the Moon on April 1, marking the first crewed lunar mission since Apollo 17 in 1972. The Artemis II mission lifted off from Kennedy Space Center aboard the Space Launch System at 6:35 p.m. EDT. The crew — Reid Wiseman, Victor Glover, Christina Koch, and Canadian astronaut Jeremy Hansen — will fly a 10-day journey around the far side of the Moon without landing. If they reach their planned distance, they will set a new record for the farthest humans have ever traveled from Earth.

Americas

Cuba Blackout; Haiti Gang Massacre

A major blackout struck western Cuba including Havana after a fault at the Antonio Guiteras power plant, leaving millions without electricity. Air France suspended all Cuba flights through mid-June due to fuel shortages. Separately, at least 70 people were killed in a gang massacre in Haiti as a U.N. force prepares to deploy to the country.

Africa

Sudan, South Sudan Violence Continues

Forty people were killed in a drone strike on funeralgoers near Abu Zabad in West Kordofan, Sudan. In South Sudan, at least 70 were killed in an attack on a gold mine. Médecins Sans Frontières reported that rape and sexual violence have become "part of everyday life" in Sudan's Darfur region, underscoring a deepening humanitarian catastrophe.

Local & National
Protest

'No Kings' Draws 8 Million — Including 4,000+ in Gainesville

Largest single-day protest in U.S. history; over 50 events across Georgia

More than 8 million Americans participated in the third wave of "No Kings" protests on Saturday, March 28, making it the largest single-day demonstration in U.S. history. Over 3,300 events took place across all 50 states, with nearly half held in Republican-leaning areas.

Georgia hosted over 50 events, including a turnout of over 4,000 in Gainesville. The flagship rally at the Minnesota State Capitol featured Bruce Springsteen, Sen. Bernie Sanders, Jane Fonda, and Joan Baez. Demonstrators voiced opposition to the Iran war, ICE enforcement, rising living costs, and democratic backsliding.

Supreme Court

SCOTUS Rules 8–1 Against Conversion Therapy Ban

Decision on Transgender Day of Visibility threatens similar laws in 20+ states

The Supreme Court ruled March 31 that Colorado's conversion therapy ban for LGBTQ youth likely violates the First Amendment. The 8-1 decision — with Justices Sotomayor and Kagan joining the majority — found the law impermissibly regulates speech based on viewpoint when applied to talk therapy.

The sole dissenter, Justice Jackson, warned the ruling undermines states' ability to regulate medical care. Every major American medical association has declared the practice harmful. The ruling is expected to pressure similar laws in over 20 states.

Technology

Meta & YouTube Found Liable; SpaceX Files Record IPO

A California jury on March 25 found Meta and YouTube negligent in designing addictive platforms, awarding $6 million to plaintiff K.G.M. Meta bears 70% of responsibility. The verdict came one day after a New Mexico jury ordered Meta to pay $375 million in a separate child exploitation case.

Meanwhile, SpaceX filed confidentially for what could be the largest IPO in history on April 1, targeting a $1.75 trillion valuation and up to $75 billion raise. The filing follows SpaceX's merger with Musk's xAI and puts it on track for a June listing — ahead of planned IPOs from OpenAI and Anthropic.

Markets

Whiplash Week: Tuesday Rally Erased by Thursday Selloff

Markets surged on Tuesday on reports Trump might end Iran hostilities — the S&P posted its best day since May. But Trump's primetime address on Wednesday offered no exit plan and signaled escalation, triggering a sharp reversal Thursday. Dow futures dropped 670 points and the S&P fell 1.6%.

U.S. gasoline crossed $4/gallon nationally. Crude oil remains above $103. The S&P 500 closed out its worst quarter since late 2022. Maersk and other shipping firms have suspended Strait of Hormuz transits indefinitely.
