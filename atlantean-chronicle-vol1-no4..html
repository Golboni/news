Iran Peace Talks Stall as Trump Cancels Pakistan Mission; Negotiations Now "By Phone"

President scraps Witkoff/Kushner trip after Iranian foreign minister leaves Islamabad — "We have all the cards" — talks continue from a distance as Strait of Hormuz traffic remains near-standstill
Compiled from wire reports  ·  April 26, 2026

Hopes for a near-term breakthrough in Iran ceasefire talks dimmed sharply this weekend after President Trump cancelled a planned trip by Special Envoy Steve Witkoff and Jared Kushner to Islamabad, citing a deal proposal from Iran "that should have been better." The cancellation came hours after Iranian Foreign Minister Abbas Araghchi departed Pakistan, ending two visits in 48 hours without face-to-face US engagement.

"We're not going to spend 15 hours in airplanes all the time going back and forth to be giving a document that was not good enough," Trump told reporters at Palm Beach International Airport. "We have all the cards. They can call us anytime they want." The president suggested talks would now continue by phone — a significant downgrade from the structured Pakistan-mediated negotiations earlier in April.

Araghchi continued his regional tour Sunday, meeting the Sultan of Oman in Muscat before returning to Islamabad and then traveling to Russia. Tehran has refused to return to direct negotiations until the US lifts its naval blockade of Iranian ports — a condition Washington has not entertained.

The diplomatic stall comes against a backdrop of severe disruption. The US Navy has redirected 37 ships under what the Treasury Department calls its "Economic Fury" sanctions campaign, and a Pentagon assessment shared with Congress in closed-door briefings indicates clearing Iranian-laid mines from the Strait of Hormuz could take up to six months. Vessel traffic through the strait remains a fraction of pre-war levels.

"We have all the cards. If they want to talk, they can come to us, or they can call us." — President Trump, April 26

In a separate development, Trump and British Prime Minister Keir Starmer discussed the "urgent need" to reopen sea lanes through Hormuz, according to Downing Street. The conversation underscored European concern about extended energy disruption — Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth had earlier dismissed European involvement, telling reporters allies "need the Strait of Hormuz much more than we do."

Iran's President Masoud Pezeshkian told Pakistan's Prime Minister Shehbaz Sharif on Sunday that Tehran "would not enter forced negotiations under pressure, threats, or a blockade." Lebanese Public Ministry of Health figures meanwhile show the Lebanon death toll has risen to at least 2,496 since March 2 — a steep jump from the April 17 update, even as the US-brokered ceasefire technically remains in effect.

Adding complexity to the diplomatic moment: a shooting incident at the White House Correspondents' Dinner Saturday night where Trump was rushed off-stage by Secret Service after gunshots rang out. Asked whether the war could have motivated the shooter, Trump replied, "I don't think so, but you never know."

Polymarket prices the probability of Strait of Hormuz traffic returning to normal by April's end at low single digits, down sharply from earlier in the month.

Domestic · Security

Security Incident at White House Correspondents' Dinner

Gunshots ring out at Washington Hilton; President rushed from stage by Secret Service; suspect in custody — motive under investigation
April 26, 2026

President Trump was rushed off-stage by Secret Service agents Saturday night after gunshots rang out during the White House Correspondents' Dinner at the Washington Hilton. The president was unharmed and a suspect is in custody, though investigators have not yet released a motive.

"I don't think so, but you never know," Trump told reporters when asked if the Iran war could have been a factor. Israeli PM Benjamin Netanyahu, Pakistani Foreign Minister Ishaq Dar, and other foreign officials issued statements expressing relief that Trump, the Vice President, and the First Lady were unharmed. Dar, mediating between Iran and the US, called the shooting "a cowardly incident" and condemned violence as "the enemy of diplomacy." Further details are expected as the investigation proceeds.

Markets & Economy

Tillis Drops Warsh Blockade After DOJ Ends Powell Probe

Senate Banking Committee votes Wednesday — Warsh on track to lead Fed before Powell term ends May 15; Polymarket prices 98% confirmation
April 26, 2026

The North Carolina Republican who had stalled Kevin Warsh's nomination to lead the Federal Reserve dropped his opposition Sunday, all but assuring confirmation of President Trump's pick before Chair Jerome Powell's term ends May 15. Senator Thom Tillis announced the move on NBC's "Meet the Press" after the Justice Department ended its criminal investigation into Powell on Friday.

Tillis had called the DOJ probe — focused on cost overruns in the Fed's headquarters renovation — a "vindictive prosecution" that threatened the central bank's independence. He told reporters he received assurances from the DOJ that "the case is completely and fully settled." The Senate Banking Committee is set to vote on Warsh's nomination Wednesday, April 29.

Polymarket traders price the probability of Warsh's confirmation at 98%, with the April 28-29 FOMC meeting carrying near-certain odds (99.7%) of no rate change. Powell can remain on the Fed Board of Governors through January 2028 even after stepping down as chair, and his lawyers have indicated he intends to do so to "defend the Federal Reserve's independence."

Regional & World

Georgia Wildfires Now Most Destructive in State History

April 26, 2026

Two massive wildfires in south Georgia have scorched more than 38,500 acres and destroyed over 120 homes, with Governor Brian Kemp telling reporters Friday that the fires have burned more homes than any other in Georgia's history. The Highway 82 Fire in Brantley County alone has destroyed at least 87 homes, with the Pineland Road Fire in Clinch and Echols counties claiming another 35.

Officials believe the Brantley fire was ignited by a foil party balloon landing on a live power line. A State of Emergency now covers 91 South Georgia counties, with a 30-day burn ban in effect. Smoke has drifted as far as Atlanta and Savannah, triggering air quality warnings. North Georgia residents are urged to monitor NWS advisories — the Gainesville forecast shows only spotty showers through the week, insufficient to alleviate drought stress.

Highway Bombing Kills 20 in Southwest Colombia

April 26, 2026

The death toll from a Saturday bus bombing on the Pan-American Highway in Cajibio, Cauca region, rose to 20 by Sunday with at least 36 wounded, including five children. The attack — described by Colombia's army chief as a "terrorist act" — was blamed on a FARC dissident faction led by Néstor Gregorio Vera Fernández, known as "Iván Mordisco."

President Gustavo Petro called for "the maximum worldwide pursuit" of the group, citing 26 separate terrorist actions across Cauca and Valle del Cauca over a 48-hour stretch. Cauca and Valle del Cauca serve as critical hubs for armed groups vying for sea and river access routes to the port of Buenaventura, a key drug-trafficking transit point. The government has offered a $1M+ reward for information leading to "Marlon," identified as the regional dissident leader.
